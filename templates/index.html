21st Century Significant Volcanic Eruptions

This site showcases data relating to significant volcanic eruptions that have occured between 2000 and 2022. As defined by the NOAA National Centers for Environmental Information, a significant eruption meets at least one of the following:

  • Moderate damange of approximately USD $1 million or more
  • Caused fatalities
  • Produced an eruption with a Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI) of 6 or larger
  • Was associated with a major earthquake
Volcanic Explosivity Index (VEI) is a relative measure of the explosiveness of eruptions that goes from 0 at the lowest and 8 at the highest. It's a logarithmic scale that looks at how much volcanic material is ejecected, to what height, and how long the eruption lasts.
